## Risk Management Standards

## Purpose

Every project involves uncertainty.

The purpose of risk management is not to eliminate all risks, but to identify them early, evaluate their potential impact, and implement appropriate mitigation strategies.

Risk management enables Code and Core to make informed decisions while maintaining quality, security, and long-term sustainability.

## Risk Management Principles

Code and Core follows these principles:

- Proactive rather than reactive.
- Evidence-based decision making.
- Shared responsibility.
- Continuous monitoring.
- Transparency.
- Proportional response.
- Continuous improvement.

## Risk Response Strategies

## Avoid

Change the plan to eliminate the risk.

---

## Reduce

Lower the probability or impact.

---

## Transfer

Shift responsibility to another party where appropriate.

---

## Accept

Accept the risk when mitigation is not practical and the impact is manageable.

---

## Roles & Responsibilities

## Leadership

- Approve risk policies.
- Review major organizational risks.

## Project Leads

- Identify project risks.
- Maintain mitigation plans.

## Contributors

- Report risks early.
- Follow approved processes.

## Reviewers

- Verify that significant risks have been evaluated.

---

## Risk Register

Every significant project should maintain a Risk Register including:

- Risk ID
- Description
- Category
- Likelihood
- Impact
- Priority
- Owner
- Mitigation Plan
- Current Status
- Review Date

---

## Monitoring & Review

Risks should be reviewed:

- During planning.
- Before major releases.
- During project retrospectives.
- After significant incidents.
- During scheduled operational reviews.

---

## Incident Response

When a significant risk becomes an issue:

1. Contain the impact.
2. Assess severity.
3. Notify stakeholders.
4. Implement corrective actions.
5. Conduct a root cause analysis.
6. Document lessons learned.
7. Update standards if necessary.

---

## Continuous Improvement

Risk management improves through:

- Lessons learned.
- Retrospectives.
- Incident reviews.
- Updated mitigation strategies.
- Regular audits.
