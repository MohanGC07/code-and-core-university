## ADR-005: Audience-First Documentation

## Status

Accepted

---

## Context

Documentation serves diverse audiences, including leadership, instructors, learners, developers, contributors, and partners.

A single writing style without considering the intended audience reduces clarity and usefulness.

---

## Decision

Every official document must explicitly identify its intended audience and be written to meet that audience's needs.

Authors should select language, examples, and technical depth appropriate to the readers.

---

## Rationale

Audience-focused documentation:

- Improves comprehension.
- Reduces ambiguity.
- Supports effective learning.
- Improves usability.
- Makes documentation easier to maintain.

---

## Consequences

## Positive

- Better communication.
- More effective documentation.
- Improved onboarding.
- Greater reader satisfaction.

## Negative

- Authors must consider audience needs during writing.

---

## Alternatives Considered

## Generic Documentation for Everyone

Rejected because it often becomes either too technical or too simplistic for many readers.

---

## Implementation

Each document should define:

- Audience
- Purpose

within its metadata.
