## Academic Standards

---

## Purpose

The Academic Standards establish the minimum quality expectations for every academic program, instructor, learner, assessment, and project delivered by Code and Core University.

These standards ensure a consistent, high-quality learning experience from Grade 5 to Grade 12.

---

## Educational Principles

Every learning experience should be:

- Student-centered
- Project-based
- Practical
- Interactive
- Ethical
- Inclusive
- Industry-informed
- Future-ready

---

## Learning Model

Every lesson follows this sequence:

1. Inspire
2. Explain
3. Demonstrate
4. Practice
5. Build
6. Reflect
7. Improve
8. Share

Students should spend more time creating than listening.

---

## Practical Learning Ratio

| Activity | Percentage |
| ----------- | -----------: |
| Theory | 30% |
| Practical Activities | 70% |

---

## Curriculum Standards

Every curriculum should include:

- Clear learning outcomes
- Age-appropriate content
- Hands-on activities
- Mini projects
- Major projects
- Reflection activities
- Portfolio evidence
- Assessment criteria

---

## Lesson Standards

Every lesson should contain:

- Learning Objectives
- Prerequisites
- Key Concepts
- Demonstration
- Guided Practice
- Independent Practice
- Challenge Activity
- Reflection
- Homework
- Portfolio Evidence

---

## Project Standards

Every project should:

- Solve a meaningful problem
- Match the learner's grade level
- Encourage creativity
- Promote teamwork when appropriate
- Be documented
- Be presented to others

Projects should focus on learning rather than competition.

---

## Portfolio Standards

Every learner maintains a digital portfolio containing:

- Programming Projects
- Robotics Projects
- AI Projects
- Documentation
- Reflection Journals
- Presentation Materials
- GitHub Repositories (when introduced)
- Certificates

The portfolio grows throughout Grades 5–12.

---

## Assessment Standards

Assessment should measure:

- Understanding
- Application
- Creativity
- Problem Solving
- Collaboration
- Communication
- Professional Conduct

Assessment methods include:

- Practical Labs
- Coding Exercises
- Robotics Demonstrations
- Quizzes
- Team Projects
- Presentations
- Portfolio Reviews
- Capstone Projects

---

## Instructor Standards

Every instructor should:

- Create a safe learning environment
- Encourage curiosity
- Provide constructive feedback
- Respect different learning styles
- Promote collaboration
- Model ethical technology use
- Continue professional development

---

## Student Standards

Learners are expected to:

- Participate actively
- Complete projects honestly
- Respect classmates and instructors
- Care for equipment
- Practice digital citizenship
- Build and maintain their portfolio

---

## Artificial Intelligence Standards

AI should be used responsibly.

Students should:

- Understand how AI works at an age-appropriate level
- Verify AI-generated information
- Credit AI assistance when appropriate
- Protect personal information
- Avoid plagiarism

AI supports learning but does not replace critical thinking.

---

## Robotics Laboratory Standards

Students should:

- Follow instructor guidance
- Handle electronic components safely
- Keep workspaces organized
- Test projects responsibly
- Report damaged equipment

Safety is always the first priority.

---

## Parent Engagement Standards

Parents are encouraged to:

- Review student portfolios
- Attend project showcases
- Support learning at home
- Encourage curiosity
- Celebrate progress

---

## Continuous Improvement

Academic standards are reviewed annually using:

- Student feedback
- Parent feedback
- Instructor feedback
- Academic research
- Industry developments
