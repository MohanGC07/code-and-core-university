## Grade 8 Projects & Innovation Framework

## Purpose

Project-Based Learning (PBL) is the core instructional approach in Grade 8. Students apply programming, web development, artificial intelligence, robotics, and software engineering concepts to solve authentic problems through collaborative engineering projects.

---

## Project Philosophy

Projects should enable students to:

- Solve meaningful problems.
- Think like engineers.
- Apply classroom knowledge.
- Work collaboratively.
- Develop creativity.
- Build professional documentation.
- Present technical solutions confidently.

---

## Engineering Design Process

Every project follows the Code and Core Engineering Cycle.

```text
Identify Problem
        ↓
Research
        ↓
Plan
        ↓
Design
        ↓
Build
        ↓
Test
        ↓
Improve
        ↓
Document
        ↓
Present
```

## Innovation Capstone

## Theme - Innovation Capstone

## Intelligent Technology Solution

Students integrate programming, AI concepts, robotics, and engineering principles into a comprehensive solution.

### Suggested Projects - Innovation Capstone

- Smart Classroom
- AI Study Assistant
- Smart Home Prototype
- Smart Attendance System

---

## Team Roles

Each team member should have a defined responsibility.

|Role|Responsibilities|
|----|----------------|
|Project Manager|Planning and coordination|
|Software Developer|Programming and implementation|
|Hardware Engineer|Robotics and electronics|
|UI Designer|User interface and usability|
|Documentation Lead|Reports and presentations|

Students are encouraged to rotate roles across projects.

---

## Project Milestones

|Milestone|Description|
|---------|-----------|
|Proposal|Define the problem and proposed solution|
|Research|Gather background information|
|Design|Prepare software and hardware designs|
|Development|Build the solution|
|Testing|Identify and resolve issues|
|Documentation|Produce technical reports|
|Presentation|Demonstrate the completed project|

---

## Documentation Requirements

Every project should include:

- Project Title
- Problem Statement
- Objectives
- Requirements
- System Design
- Source Code
- Circuit Diagram (if applicable)
- Testing Results
- Challenges
- Improvements
- Reflection

---

## Assessment Criteria

|Criterion|Weight|
|---------|------|
|Technical Implementation|30%|
|Problem Solving|20%|
|Innovation|15%|
|Documentation|15%|
|Team Collaboration|10%|
|Presentation|10%|

---

## Innovation Expectations

Students should demonstrate:

- Creativity
- Critical Thinking
- Engineering Design
- User-Centered Development
- Ethical Technology Use
- Continuous Improvement

---

## Portfolio Requirements

Students should add the following to their digital portfolio:

- Project Proposal
- Planning Documents
- Source Code
- Screenshots
- Circuit Diagrams
- Testing Evidence
- Presentation Slides
- Reflection Journal

---

## Teacher Responsibilities

Teachers should:

- Approve project ideas.
- Guide project planning.
- Monitor progress.
- Provide regular feedback.
- Encourage innovation.
- Assess fairly using the rubric.
- Ensure safe laboratory practices.

---

## Student Responsibilities

Students are expected to:

- Participate actively.
- Meet project deadlines.
- Communicate effectively.
- Respect team members.
- Maintain project documentation.
- Test their solutions thoroughly.
- Reflect on their learning.

---

## Success Indicators

Successful projects should:

- Solve a clearly defined problem.
- Demonstrate technical competency.
- Be functional and reliable.
- Include complete documentation.
- Show creativity and innovation.
- Be presented professionally.
