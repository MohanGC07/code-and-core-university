## Grade 7 Parent Guide

## Purpose

Parents and guardians play a critical role in a student's academic and personal development. This guide explains what students learn during Grade 7 and how families can actively support their growth.

---

## Grade 7 Vision

Grade 7 is where students begin thinking like young engineers.

Rather than simply using technology, learners begin to:

- Build software
- Design websites
- Explore Artificial Intelligence
- Develop robotics projects
- Solve real-world problems
- Work collaboratively
- Document professional work

The emphasis is on practical learning rather than memorization.

---

## What Your Child Will Learn

During Grade 7, students will study:

| Learning Area           | Focus                                        |
|-------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| Python Programming      | Intermediate programming and problem solving |
| Web Development         | HTML and CSS                                 |
| Artificial Intelligence | AI concepts and responsible AI               |
| Robotics                | Arduino programming and sensors              |
| Computer Systems        | Hardware and networking fundamentals         |
| Software Engineering    | Documentation and project organization       |

---

## Learning Approach

Students learn through:

- Hands-on activities
- Coding exercises
- Laboratory sessions
- Team projects
- Engineering challenges
- Reflection activities
- Presentations

Approximately:

| Activity           | Percentage |
|--------------------|-----------:|
| Theory             | 30%        |
| Practical Learning | 70%        |

---

## Skills Students Develop

By the end of Grade 7, students are expected to develop:

## Technical Skills

- Programming
- Website development
- Robotics
- AI awareness
- Digital literacy

## Professional Skills

- Communication
- Teamwork
- Creativity
- Problem solving
- Time management
- Documentation
- Presentation

---

## Student Responsibilities

Students are expected to:

- Attend classes regularly.
- Complete assignments on time.
- Participate actively.
- Respect classmates and teachers.
- Maintain organized project files.
- Practice ethical technology use.
- Follow laboratory safety rules.

---

## Parent Responsibilities

Parents can support learning by:

- Encouraging regular study habits.
- Asking about classroom projects.
- Celebrating effort and progress.
- Providing a quiet study environment.
- Monitoring healthy technology use.
- Encouraging curiosity and creativity.
- Communicating with teachers when needed.

---

## Supporting Learning at Home

Parents are encouraged to:

- Ask students to explain what they learned.
- Encourage independent problem solving.
- Discuss technology used in daily life.
- Support project completion.
- Review portfolio updates together.

The goal is not to provide answers but to encourage thinking and exploration.

---

## Homework

Homework may include:

- Programming exercises
- Website improvements
- Robotics documentation
- Reflection journals
- Project planning
- Research activities

Parents should encourage consistent effort while allowing students to complete their own work.

---

## Assessment

Student progress is evaluated through:

| Assessment Type | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Classroom Activities | Daily participation |
| Coding Exercises | Programming practice |
| Laboratory Work | Practical skills |
| Mini Projects | Applied learning |
| Semester Projects | Engineering challenges |
| Portfolio | Continuous evidence of learning |
| Presentations | Communication skills |

Assessment focuses on growth, application, and continuous improvement.

---

## Student Portfolio

Every student maintains a digital portfolio containing:

- Programming projects
- Websites
- Robotics projects
- AI activities
- Documentation
- Reflection journals
- Presentations

Parents are encouraged to review the portfolio periodically with their child.

---

## Artificial Intelligence

Students learn:

- What AI is
- How AI is used responsibly
- Benefits of AI
- Risks of AI
- Ethical decision-making

The curriculum promotes responsible, ethical, and critical use of AI technologies.

---

## Robotics & Laboratory Safety

During robotics lessons, students learn to:

- Handle electronic components safely.
- Follow classroom safety procedures.
- Respect laboratory equipment.
- Work responsibly in teams.

Safety is a core part of every practical lesson.

---

## Attendance

Regular attendance is essential because many lessons involve practical classroom activities that cannot easily be replicated outside the classroom.

Parents should notify the school whenever a student is absent.

---

## Communication

Parents can communicate with teachers regarding:

- Academic progress
- Student wellbeing
- Attendance
- Classroom behavior
- Project support
- Learning challenges

Open communication helps ensure student success.

---

## Frequently Asked Questions

## Does my child need previous programming experience?

No. The curriculum is designed to build knowledge progressively.

---

## Will students use Artificial Intelligence?

Yes. Students learn AI concepts responsibly while developing critical thinking and ethical awareness.

---

## Are projects completed individually or in teams?

Students complete both individual and collaborative projects throughout the academic year.

---

## How can I help if I am not a technology expert?

Encourage curiosity, ask questions about your child's work, celebrate progress, and communicate with teachers when support is needed.

---

## Success Indicators

Parents can expect students to:

- Demonstrate greater confidence with technology.
- Build practical software projects.
- Develop engineering thinking.
- Improve communication skills.
- Become more independent learners.
- Show responsibility and professionalism.
