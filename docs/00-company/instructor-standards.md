## Instructor Standards

## Purpose

This document defines the professional standards expected of every instructor at Code and Core University.

The purpose of these standards is to ensure that every learner experiences consistent, high-quality instruction regardless of the course, instructor, learning format, or delivery platform.

These standards apply to full-time instructors, part-time instructors, mentors, guest instructors, workshop facilitators, corporate trainers, and teaching assistants.

---

## Relationship to Other Documents

This document builds upon the educational foundation established in previous company documents.

- Mission Statement defines why Code and Core exists.
- Vision Statement defines where the organization is heading.
- Teaching Philosophy defines how learning is approached.
- Learning Methodology (CCLF) defines how learners progress.
- Course Design Standards define how educational content is structured.

Instructor Standards define how instructors deliver that learning experience.

## Core Responsibilities

Every instructor is expected to:

- Deliver high-quality instruction.
- Create an engaging learning environment.
- Encourage curiosity and independent thinking.
- Demonstrate professionalism.
- Support learner growth.
- Model lifelong learning.
- Represent the values of Code and Core.

---

## Teaching Excellence

Technical expertise is essential, but it is only one part of excellent teaching.

A Code and Core instructor combines:

- Technical knowledge
- Clear communication
- Practical demonstrations
- Patience
- Professionalism
- Mentorship
- Continuous improvement

The objective is not simply to explain concepts but to help learners become capable and confident professionals.

---

## The Instructor as a Learning Facilitator

The instructor's primary responsibility is to guide learners through the learning process.

Instructors should:

- Encourage exploration.
- Ask thoughtful questions.
- Demonstrate practical solutions.
- Support problem-solving.
- Build learner confidence.
- Gradually reduce dependence by fostering independent thinking.

The goal is to develop learners who can continue learning long after the course has ended.

---

## Standard Lesson Preparation

Every instructional session should be prepared before delivery.

Preparation includes:

- Reviewing lesson objectives.
- Testing all code examples.
- Verifying hardware and software.
- Preparing datasets and project files.
- Confirming software versions.
- Anticipating common learner questions.
- Preparing demonstrations.
- Ensuring all learning materials are accessible.

Preparation follows a simple cycle:

```text
Prepare
    ↓
Verify
    ↓
Deliver
    ↓
Reflect
    ↓
Improve
```

---

## Responding to Learner Questions

Instructors should encourage learners to think before providing answers.

A typical sequence is:

1. Understand the learner's current thinking.
2. Ask guiding questions.
3. Encourage investigation and experimentation.
4. Provide progressively more direct guidance when appropriate.

Examples of guiding questions include:

- What have you tried so far?
- What do you expect this code to do?
- What does the error message suggest?
- Which concept applies here?
- What would you test next?

The objective is to develop independent problem-solvers.

---

## Growth-Oriented Feedback

Feedback should promote continuous improvement.

Effective feedback includes:

1. Recognize specific strengths.
2. Identify specific improvements.
3. Provide actionable recommendations.
4. Encourage the learner's next step.

Feedback should always be:

- Specific
- Respectful
- Actionable
- Timely
- Encouraging

Generic praise should be avoided in favor of meaningful observations that help learners understand what they did well and how they can improve.

---

## Professional and Respectful Learning Environment

Every instructor is responsible for creating a classroom culture built on respect and high expectations.

Instructors should:

- Treat every learner with dignity and professionalism.
- Encourage curiosity and thoughtful questions.
- Welcome mistakes as learning opportunities.
- Promote collaboration.
- Maintain academic honesty.
- Foster inclusive participation.
- Challenge learners while providing appropriate support.

A respectful environment helps learners develop confidence without lowering standards.

---

## Continuous Professional Development (CPD)

Learning is a lifelong responsibility for every instructor.

Instructors are expected to continuously improve both technical expertise and teaching practice.

Professional development may include:

- Learning new technologies.
- Improving instructional methods.
- Building personal or open-source projects.
- Reading technical books, research papers, and documentation.
- Attending workshops, conferences, or webinars.
- Sharing knowledge through articles, talks, or videos.
- Reflecting on learner feedback and refining future lessons.
- Mentoring other instructors.

Professional growth is measured by meaningful improvement rather than time spent learning.

---

## Instructor Code of Conduct

Every instructor is expected to:

- Act with honesty and integrity.
- Respect learner privacy.
- Communicate professionally.
- Prepare thoroughly.
- Teach accurately.
- Admit mistakes openly and correct them promptly.
- Continue learning.
- Represent Code and Core positively.

- Stronger educational outcomes.
- Professional classroom culture.
- Scalable instructor onboarding.
- Continuous organizational improvement.

## Implementation Examples

## YouTube Lesson

- Well-prepared examples.
- Clear explanations.
- Guided questions.
- Practical demonstration.
- Encouragement for independent practice.

## Robotics Workshop

- Hardware tested in advance.
- Safety instructions provided.
- Learners troubleshoot before receiving solutions.
- Instructor offers guidance through questioning.
- Reflection at the end of the session.

## Corporate Training

- Customized examples relevant to the audience.
- Interactive discussions.
- Practical exercises.
- Continuous feedback.
- Professional facilitation.

Although the teaching environments differ, the instructor standards remain the same.
